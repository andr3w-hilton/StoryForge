# Black Flag Running — Cover Art Prompts for Gemini

---

## Style Reference Notes (for both images)

All StoryForge covers share the same visual language:
- Digital painted illustration — detailed, textured, not photorealistic and not cartoon
- Protagonist always viewed from behind or partial profile — face never shown
- Deep atmospheric perspective with a strong single light source
- Dark, dramatic mood with rich saturated colour
- Fighting Fantasy / old-school gamebook illustration aesthetic updated with modern digital painting quality

---

## Image 1 — Book Cover

**Aspect ratio:** Portrait, approximately 2:3 (e.g. 800×1200px)
**Title text:** Render the title directly into the image at the top.

### Prompt

Digital painted illustration in the style of a Fighting Fantasy gamebook cover. Portrait orientation, 2:3 aspect ratio.

A lone figure stands on the weathered dock of Nassau harbour, viewed from behind and slightly to the right, looking out over the water. The figure is clearly a ship's captain — long coat, cutlass at the hip, a weathered confidence in the set of the shoulders. They are still. Watching.

The harbour stretches before them. Nassau is alive behind and around them — the silhouette of the fort on the cliffs to the left, the crowded waterfront of a pirate republic: stripped rigging, market stalls, the hulls of a dozen ships pressed together. The town feels inhabited and precarious, caught between defiance and dread.

On the horizon, dead centre in the composition, three large Crown warships sit in line across the mouth of the harbour. They are not attacking. They are waiting. Their sails are furled. Their flags — dark against the dawn sky — hang still. They are the threat the captain is reading.

The light is early morning — a low golden-amber dawn breaking behind the warships, silhouetting them against a pale sky that grades from warm gold at the horizon to deep blue-grey overhead. The sea reflects the dawn in broken copper and grey. The dock planks are wet with morning dew.

Colour palette: warm weathered gold and amber at the horizon, deep blue-grey sea and sky, the dark silhouettes of the warships, salt-bleached wood on the docks. The overall tone is tense and watchful — a moment of quiet before something breaks.

At the top of the image, render the title text: **"BLACK FLAG RUNNING"** in a bold, weathered serif display font — aged and salt-worn, like lettering carved into a ship's hull or stamped into a wanted notice. The text should be cream or off-white, slightly worn at the edges, integrated into the illustration rather than overlaid — as if it belongs to the image. The title sits above the scene, clear and legible against the darker upper sky. No subtitle. No author name. No other text.

## Image 2 — Hero Image (Widescreen)

**Aspect ratio:** Landscape, approximately 16:9 (e.g. 1920×1080px)
**No title text space needed** — this image is used as a full-width banner, no text will be composited.

### Prompt

Digital painted illustration in the style of a Fighting Fantasy gamebook interior spread. Landscape orientation, 16:9 aspect ratio. Wider and more panoramic than a cover — the world is bigger, the figure smaller in frame.

Nassau harbour at dawn, viewed from the waterfront. The same captain figure from the cover — long coat, cutlass at the hip — stands in the lower-left third of the composition, viewed from behind, looking out across the harbour. They are a presence in the scene, not the subject of it.

The full sweep of Nassau is visible: the fort on the cliffs, the dense waterfront of the pirate republic with its taverns and market stalls and ships in various states of repair, the smell of the place almost tangible in the painted detail — tar, salt, wood smoke. Other figures move in the background, going about harbour business, but there is a tension in how they move. People watching the horizon without looking like they're watching the horizon.

Across the mouth of the harbour, the three Crown warships sit in blockade formation — larger and more prominent in this wider composition than on the cover. Their scale relative to Nassau's ships is clear. They are patient and overwhelming. One flies a large Crown flag that catches a hint of breeze. The other two are still.

The light is the same dawn amber, but in the widescreen format the sky takes up more of the frame — a dramatic expanse of pale gold-to-blue that makes the warships feel like a judgement sitting on the horizon.

Colour palette: the same warm-gold-and-steel-grey as the cover, but with more room for the blue of open water, the brown-gold of Nassau's wooden buildings, and the grey-white of the Crown ships' hulls. Rich and specific — not generic ocean blue.

No text. No title. No UI elements. Full bleed illustration.
